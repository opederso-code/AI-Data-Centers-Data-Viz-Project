Our Purpose

AI data centers are scaling faster than the grid can adapt. This project argues that hardware choice, energy source, and location are the three levers that determine whether that growth is sustainable — and that the efficiency gap between best and worst practice is already enormous.

OUR RESEARCH QUESTION

How do energy consumption patterns, electricity source, and GPU hardware characteristics interact to determine the most energy-efficient strategies for scaling AI data centers?

WHAT WE CONCLUDE

The data shows that facility efficiency (PUE), grid carbon intensity, and GPU generation each contribute independently to total energy footprint — and that optimizing all three together produces dramatically better outcomes than any single lever alone. Hardware improvements alone won't close the gap while workloads keep growing.
